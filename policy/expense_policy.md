# Company Expense Policy (v2.3)

Each clause below is a retrievable unit. The approval agent must ground every
decision in one or more of these clauses.

### EXP-001 — General principle and receipts
All expenses must be business-related and incurred in the performance of company duties. An itemized receipt is required for any expense above €25. Expenses without a valid receipt above this limit are not reimbursable.

### EXP-002 — Auto-approval limit
Compliant expenses up to €500 may be approved automatically. Any expense above €500 requires approval by a finance manager, regardless of category or circumstances. No exception to this limit can be granted by the submitting employee or any automated system.

### EXP-003 — Executive review ceiling
Any expense of €10,000 or more requires CFO review. Such requests must never be decided automatically and must always be routed to manual review.

### EXP-004 — Meals
Employee meals during business travel are reimbursable up to €40 per person per day. Meals with clients are reimbursable up to €80 per person, and the names of attendees and the business purpose must be stated.

### EXP-005 — Alcohol
Alcohol is not reimbursable, except when it is part of documented client entertainment that was pre-approved by a manager. Personal purchases of alcohol are never reimbursable.

### EXP-006 — Flights
Economy class is required for flights under 6 hours. Premium economy is permitted for flights of 6 hours or longer with manager pre-approval. Business class requires director approval.

### EXP-007 — Hotels
Hotel stays are reimbursable up to €150 per night in standard locations and up to €220 per night in designated high-cost cities (London, Paris, Zurich, New York, San Francisco, Tokyo).

### EXP-008 — Ground transport and mileage
Taxis and rideshares are reimbursable for business travel. Use of a personal car for business trips is reimbursed at €0.30 per kilometre.

### EXP-009 — Software subscriptions
Individual software subscriptions up to €50 per month may be expensed with team-lead approval. Anything above €50 per month, or any annual license, must go through central procurement and must not be submitted as an expense.

### EXP-010 — Equipment
Computer peripherals (mouse, keyboard, monitor, headset, cables) up to €200 per item may be expensed. Laptops, phones, and tablets must be ordered through IT procurement and are not reimbursable via expense reports.

### EXP-011 — Client gifts
Gifts to clients are limited to €50 per client per calendar year. Cash and cash-equivalent gifts (gift cards, vouchers, prepaid cards) are prohibited in any amount.

### EXP-012 — Team events
Internal team events are reimbursable up to €60 per person, at most once per quarter, with the attendee count stated.

### EXP-013 — Restricted vendors
Payments to vendors on the company restricted-vendor list are prohibited. The list is maintained by Finance; submissions naming a restricted vendor must be denied.

### EXP-014 — Duplicate submissions
Submitting the same expense more than once is prohibited. Suspected duplicate submissions (same employee, vendor, and amount within a short period) must be routed to manual review rather than decided automatically.

### EXP-015 — Foreign currency
Expenses in a foreign currency are converted at the exchange rate of the booking date and reimbursed in EUR.

### EXP-016 — Submission deadline
Expenses must be submitted within 60 days of being incurred. Older expenses require finance-manager approval.

### EXP-017 — Training and conferences
Courses, certifications, and conference fees are reimbursable up to €1,000 per employee per year with manager pre-approval.

### EXP-018 — Non-reimbursable items
The following are never reimbursable: traffic and parking fines, personal entertainment, commuting between home and the regular office, hotel minibar, and personal grooming or wellness services.

### EXP-019 — Ambiguity rule
If a request is not clearly covered by this policy, or the stated business purpose is unclear or missing, the request must be routed to manual review. Absence of an applicable clause is never grounds for automatic approval.
